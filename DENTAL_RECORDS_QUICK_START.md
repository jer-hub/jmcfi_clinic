# Dental Records System - Quick Start Guide

## For Clinic Staff

### Accessing the Dental Records System

1. **Login** to the JMCFI Clinic system with your staff/doctor account
2. Navigate to **Dental Records** from the main menu
3. You'll see a list of all patient dental records

### Creating a New Dental Record

**Step 1: Initial Patient Information**
1. Click the **"New Dental Record"** button
2. Fill in the required fields:
   - Select the **Patient** from the dropdown
   - Enter **Middle Name**
   - Fill in **Age**, **Gender**, **Civil Status**
   - Enter complete **Address**
   - Set **Date of Birth** and **Place of Birth**
   - Provide **Email** and **Contact Numbers**
   - Select **Designation** (Student or Employee)
   - Enter **Department/College/Office**
   - Add **Emergency Contact** information
   - Set **Date of Examination** (defaults to today)
   - Select **Examined By** (dentist)
3. Review the consent statement
4. Check **"Consent Form Signed"** if patient has signed
5. Click **"Create Dental Record"**

**Step 2: Complete Detailed Sections**

After creating the record, you'll be redirected to the comprehensive editor with 8 tabs:

#### Tab 1: Demographics
- Review and update patient information if needed
- Save changes with "Save Demographics" button

#### Tab 2: Health Questionnaire (Section A)
- **Hospital History:**
  - When was last hospital confinement? Why?
  - When was last doctor consultation? Why?
  - Under doctor's care last 2 years? If yes, why?

- **Health Concerns:**
  - History of excessive bleeding? When?
  - Taking medications last 2 years? What for?
  - Easily exhausted when walking?
  - Swollen ankles during the day?
  - Use more than 2 pillows? Why?
  - Diagnosis of tumor/cancer? When?

- **For Women Only:** (Show if patient is female)
  - Are you pregnant? How many months?
  - Taking birth control pills? Specify
  - Anticipate becoming pregnant?
  - Having period now?

Click **"Save Health Questionnaire"** when complete

#### Tab 3: Systems Review (Section B)
Check all conditions that apply to the patient:

- **Cardiovascular:** Heart disease, hypertension, rheumatic heart disease, heart surgery, stroke
- **Respiratory:** Asthma, emphysema, cough, pneumonia, hay fever, sinus problem, tuberculosis
- **Blood/Hematologic:** Anemia, bleeding tendencies, hemophilia, sickle cell anemia, blood transfusion
- **Endocrine:** Diabetes, thyroid problem, glandular problem
- **Gastrointestinal:** Stomach ulcer, liver problem, hepatitis A/B
- **Renal:** Kidney problem
- **Infectious:** HIV/AIDS, scarlet fever, STD
- **Neurological:** Brain injury, psychiatric visit
- **Musculoskeletal:** Arthritis, rheumatism, TMJ problem
- **Other:** Cancer treatment, glaucoma, cold sores, bruising, drug addiction, etc.

**Important Fields:**
- List all **allergies** in the text area
- Document any **other conditions** not listed

Click **"Save Systems Review"** when complete

#### Tab 4: Dental History (Section C)
- First dental visit?
- If not, when was last dental visit? What for?
- Ever had teeth extracted? When?
- Allergy to local anesthesia? When?
- Wearing dental appliance? What type?
- Pain or discomfort now? Where?

Click **"Save Dental History"** when complete

#### Tab 5: Pediatric Info (Only for patients under 18)
Ask parent/guardian:
- What is condition of child's mouth?
- Normal pregnancy and birth?
- Drinks bottle at bedtime?
- When was last dentist visit?
- What age did first tooth appear?
- Check habits: thumb sucking, tongue thrusting, nail biting, mouth breathing, teeth grinding
- Other habits?

Click **"Save Pediatric History"** when complete

#### Tab 6: Examination
Record your clinical findings:

**Extraoral:**
- Facial symmetry & profile
- Cutaneous areas
- Lips
- Eyes
- Lymph nodes
- TMJ (Temporomandibular Joint)

**Intraoral:**
- Buccal & labial mucosa
- Gingiva
- Soft palate
- Hard palate
- Tongue
- Salivary flow
- Oral hygiene

Click **"Save Examination Findings"** when complete

#### Tab 7: Vital Signs
Record current measurements:
- Blood pressure (e.g., 120/80)
- Pulse rate (bpm)
- Respiratory rate (breaths/min)
- Temperature (°C or °F)
- Weight (kg or lbs)
- Height (cm or ft)

Click **"Save Vital Signs"** when complete

#### Tab 8: Dental Chart
Use Universal Numbering System:
- **Permanent teeth:** 1-32
- **Primary teeth:** 51-85

**To add a tooth:**
1. Enter tooth number (1-85)
2. Select tooth type (Permanent or Primary)
3. Select condition:
   - Healthy
   - Cavity/Caries
   - Filled
   - Missing
   - Extracted
   - Impacted
   - Root Canal
   - Crown
   - Bridge
   - Implant
   - Fractured
   - Other
4. Add notes if needed
5. Click **"Add Tooth"**

**To remove a tooth:** Click the red X button next to the tooth

### Searching and Filtering Records

From the Dental Records list:

**Search by:**
- Patient name
- Email address

**Filter by:**
- Date range (From - To)

Click **"Search"** to apply filters  
Click **"Reset"** to clear filters

### Viewing Record Details

1. Click the **blue eye icon** to view full record
2. See all sections in organized cards
3. Click **"Edit"** to make changes
4. Click **"Export JSON"** to download data
5. Click **"Back"** to return to list

### Exporting Data

**To export a single record:**
1. Open the record detail page
2. Click **"Export JSON"**
3. JSON file opens in new tab
4. Save or copy the data

**JSON export includes:**
- Complete patient demographics
- All health questionnaire responses
- Systems review conditions
- Vital signs
- Consent status

### Important Notes for Staff

⚠️ **High Priority Flags:**
- **Pregnant patients** - Flag for dentist review before procedures
- **Bleeding disorders** - Special precautions needed
- **Heart disease** - May need antibiotic prophylaxis
- **Diabetes** - Monitor for infection risk
- **Allergies** - Verify before any medication

✅ **Best Practices:**
- Complete all sections during patient visit
- Update dental chart after every examination
- Always verify emergency contact information
- Mark consent signed only after patient acknowledges
- Review allergies before prescribing medications

## For Patients

### Viewing Your Dental Records

1. **Login** to the JMCFI Clinic system
2. Navigate to **"My Dental Records"** from the menu
3. See a list of all your dental examinations
4. Click on any record to view complete details

**What you can see:**
- Your demographics and contact information
- Health questionnaire responses
- Medical conditions and allergies
- Vital signs from each visit
- Dental examination findings
- Your dental chart

**What you cannot do:**
- Edit records (read-only access)
- Delete records
- Export data

### Understanding Your Dental Chart

**Tooth Numbering:**
- Your teeth are numbered 1-32 (if adult)
- Primary (baby) teeth numbered 51-85

**Tooth Conditions:**
- 🟢 **Green (Healthy)** - No issues detected
- 🔴 **Red (Cavity)** - Tooth decay, needs treatment
- 🟡 **Yellow (Filled)** - Previously treated cavity
- ⚫ **Gray (Missing)** - Tooth is missing

### When to Update Information

Contact the clinic if:
- Your address or phone number changes
- You have new allergies
- You start new medications
- You become pregnant (for women)
- Your emergency contact changes
- You develop new medical conditions

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Problem:** Can't find a patient in the dropdown  
**Solution:** Make sure the patient has an account in the system. Contact admin to create one.

**Problem:** Form won't save  
**Solution:** Check for required fields (marked with *). Ensure all dates are in correct format.

**Problem:** Can't add tooth to dental chart  
**Solution:** Verify tooth number is valid (1-32 for permanent, 51-85 for primary)

**Problem:** Export JSON shows "null" values  
**Solution:** This is normal for unfilled sections. Only completed data shows actual values.
